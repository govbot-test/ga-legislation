26 LC 112 4281
House Resolution 51EX
122nd, Reese of the 140th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Urging Governor Brian P. Kemp to immediately release funds withheld through executive
2 budget disregards issued against House Bill 974, the Fiscal Year 2027 General
3 Appropriations Act; affirming the exclusive constitutional authority of the General Assembly
4 over the appropriation of public funds; recognizing the bipartisan effort of House and Senate
5 leadership in crafting a balanced budget; urging the Governor to honor the will of the
6 legislature; and for other purposes.
7 WHEREAS, Article III, Section I, Paragraph I of the Georgia Constitution vests the
8 legislative power of the state in the General Assembly, including the exclusive authority to
9 appropriate public funds on behalf of the people of Georgia; and
10 WHEREAS, Article III, Section IX, Paragraph I of the Georgia Constitution provides that
11 no money shall be drawn from the state treasury except by appropriation made by law,
12 establishing the legislature as the sole branch authorized to direct public spending; and
13 WHEREAS, Article III, Section IX, Paragraph II requires the General Assembly to annually
14 appropriate funds necessary to operate all departments and agencies of the state, and it is the
15 legislature, not the executive, that holds final appropriating authority; and
H. R. 51EX
- 1 -
26 LC 112 4281
16 WHEREAS, Article V, Section I, Paragraph II charges the Governor with the duty to take
17 care that the laws are faithfully executed, a duty that expressly includes the faithful execution
18 of duly enacted appropriations acts; and
19 WHEREAS, Article III, Section IX, Paragraph VI(e) grants the Governor a constitutionally
20 defined line-item veto power over appropriations bills, which triggers the General
21 Assembly's right to override that veto by a two-thirds vote of both chambers, preserving the
22 constitutional balance of power between the branches; and
23 WHEREAS, the use of executive budget disregards to withhold appropriated spending
24 bypasses the line-item veto process entirely, strips the General Assembly of its override
25 authority, and allows one officer of the executive branch to unilaterally nullify decisions
26 made by 236 duly elected members of the legislature; and
27 WHEREAS, Article I of the Georgia Constitution requires that the legislative, judicial, and
28 executive powers remain forever separate and distinct, and that no person discharging the
29 duties of one branch shall exercise the functions of another; and
30 WHEREAS, the 2026 Legislative Session was Governor Kemp's eighth and final as
31 governor, making the integrity of the Fiscal Year 2027 appropriations process a matter of
32 lasting significance for the state's financial continuity and the orderly transition to the next
33 administration; and
34 WHEREAS, House Bill 974, the Fiscal Year 2027 General Appropriations Act, passed the
35 Georgia House of Representatives by a vote of 159 to 4, reflecting broad, bipartisan
36 consensus built through months of hearings, negotiations, and deliberation between members
37 and staff of both chambers; and
H. R. 51EX
- 2 -
26 LC 112 4281
38 WHEREAS, House Speaker Jon G. Burns and House Appropriations Committee Chairman
39 Representative Matt Hatchett led the House through a rigorous and transparent budget
40 process focused on strategic investment and fiscal responsibility; and
41 WHEREAS, Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and Senate Appropriations Committee
42 Chairman Senator Blake Tillery guided the Senate's review of the budget, working through
43 the conference committee process to reach agreement with the House on a final
44 appropriations act that reflected the collective judgment of both chambers; and
45 WHEREAS, House Minority Leader Carolyn Hugley and Senate Minority Leader Harold
46 Jones II, together with both Democratic caucuses, participated fully in the appropriations
47 process and placed the passage of a balanced, responsible budget at the top of their legislative
48 priorities; and
49 WHEREAS, the bipartisan, bicameral work that produced House Bill 974 represents
50 hundreds of hours of hearings, testimony from agencies and community stakeholders, fiscal
51 analysis, and good-faith negotiation, all of which the Governor's unilateral disregards
52 effectively nullify without legislative recourse; and
53 WHEREAS, Governor Brian Kemp, upon signing House Bill 974 on May 12, 2026,
54 simultaneously issued 157 executive budget disregards and line-item vetoes, cutting more
55 than $344 million from the budget approved by the General Assembly; and
56 WHEREAS, the Governor's stated justification was a $1.3 billion revenue shortfall created
57 by House Bill 463, an income tax reduction bill he himself signed into law on the same day,
58 meaning the budget hole he cited was one of his own making; and
H. R. 51EX
- 3 -
26 LC 112 4281
59 WHEREAS, the disregards cut more than $88 million from public education, including $30.7
60 million for school transportation, a $12 million bond veto for school safety equipment, $2.7
61 million for vision and hearing screening tied to the legislature's literacy initiative, and $2.5
62 million for out-of-school care; and
63 WHEREAS, the disregards cut more than $81 million from healthcare, including the full $48
64 million in new Medicaid reimbursement rate increases for primary care, dental, and
65 psychiatric providers, as well as $2 million for senior caregiver support services; and
66 WHEREAS, the disregards withheld approximately $15.8 million from the Department of
67 Human Services, including $9.4 million for domestic violence shelters, $3.3 million for
68 sexual assault centers, $2 million for unhoused veterans, and $1.5 million for child welfare
69 and family reunification services; and
70 WHEREAS, across the Governor's first four fiscal years, he issued 49 disregards totaling $85
71 million, then escalated to 134 disregards on $242 million in FY 2024 alone, and has now
72 issued 157 disregards on more than $344 million in FY 2027, representing a dramatic and
73 unchecked escalation of executive spending authority; and
74 WHEREAS, the practice of issuing disregards to impound appropriated funds, rather than
75 exercising the constitutionally prescribed line-item veto, denies the legislature any
76 opportunity for override, treats enacted law as advisory, and sets a precedent that threatens
77 the fundamental constitutional role of the General Assembly; and
78 WHEREAS, the Georgia House of Representatives formally rejects the use of executive
79 budget disregards as a substitute for the line-item veto and demands that appropriations
80 enacted by the legislature be treated as binding law.
H. R. 51EX
- 4 -
26 LC 112 4281
81 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
82 the members of this body urge Governor Brian P. Kemp to immediately release all funds
83 withheld through executive budget disregards issued in connection with House Bill 974 and
84 direct affected state agencies to proceed with expenditure of appropriated funds in
85 accordance with the enacted appropriations act.
86 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body affirm that the appropriation
87 of public funds is an exclusively legislative power under the Georgia Constitution, that
88 appropriations lawfully enacted through the general appropriations process carry the full
89 force of law, and that executive budget disregards do not constitute a valid or constitutional
90 exercise of executive authority.
91 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body urge the Governor to exercise
92 the constitutionally prescribed line-item veto rather than issuing disregards for any specific
93 appropriation that cannot be funded, thereby allowing the General Assembly to consider
94 exercising its override authority and preserving the proper separation of powers.
95 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body recognize the dedicated work
96 of Speaker Jon G. Burns, Representative Matt Hatchett, Minority Leader Carolyn Hugley,
97 Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, Senator Blake Tillery, Minority Leader Harold Jones II, and
98 the members and staff of both chambers whose collective effort produced a balanced and
99 responsible appropriations act.
100 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body urge the Office of the
101 Attorney General to provide a formal legal opinion on the constitutional authority of the
102 Governor to issue executive budget disregards that impound appropriated funds and to advise
103 the General Assembly on available legal remedies to enforce its appropriations authority.
H. R. 51EX
- 5 -
26 LC 112 4281
104 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body urge the House
105 Appropriations Committee and the House Judiciary Committee to develop legislation
106 establishing clear statutory limits on the Governor's authority to withhold appropriated funds
107 outside the constitutionally prescribed line-item veto process, protecting the legislature's
108 power of the purse for this and all future administrations.
109 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
110 and directed to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to
111 Governor Brian P. Kemp, the Lieutenant Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the
112 Georgia Senate, the Attorney General, the Office of Planning and Budget, the Senate
113 Appropriations Committee, the House Appropriations Committee, and the Georgia Budget
114 and Policy Institute.
