26 LC 112 4261
House Resolution 26EX
By: Representative Hagan of the 156th
A RESOLUTION
2 WHEREAS, while completing her senior year at Vidalia Heritage Academy, Anna Nalley
3 enrolled in an Emergency Medical Technician certification program through Southeastern
4 Technical College, demonstrating an early commitment to serving others; and
5 WHEREAS, despite being the youngest student in her EMT program, Anna Nalley
6 distinguished herself through her confidence, maturity, and willingness to embrace both the
7 challenges of a demanding healthcare environment and the increased workload of dual
8 enrollment; and
9 WHEREAS, she completed 94 hours of clinical experience, gaining valuable hands-on
10 exposure to emergency medical services and patient care while developing the skills
11 necessary to serve those in need; and
12 WHEREAS, through diligence, perseverance, and dedication to excellence, she earned
13 certification as a Nationally Registered Emergency Medical Technician and graduated from
14 Vidalia Heritage Academy with honors and distinction; and

15 WHEREAS, Anna Nalley has accepted a position with Emanuel County EMS, where she
16 will begin serving her community as an Emergency Medical Technician; and
17 WHEREAS, she plans to continue her education at Georgia Southern University, pursuing
18 a degree in biomedical sciences while further expanding her healthcare knowledge; and
19 WHEREAS, through her initiative, leadership, compassion, and dedication to the well-being
20 of others, Anna Nalley serves as an inspiration to her peers and a credit to her family, her

24 achievements, congratulate her upon earning certification as a Nationally Registered
25 Emergency Medical Technician, and extend best wishes for continued success.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
27 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Anna
28 Nalley.
