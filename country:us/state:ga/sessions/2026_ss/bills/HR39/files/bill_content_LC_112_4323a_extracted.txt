26 LC 112 4323
House Resolution 39EX
By: Representative Scott of the 76th
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring the life and memory of Betty Lee Harrison Dickey; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, it is with profound respect and heartfelt remembrance that we honor the life and
3 legacy of Betty Lee Harrison Dickey, whose life, though brief in years, was rich in love,
4 faith, family devotion, and cherished memories; and
5 WHEREAS, Betty Lee Harrison Dickey was born on October 7, 1938, in Havana, Florida,
6 as the beloved youngest daughter and "baby" of the late Goldie Ervin Harrison and John
7 Spencer Harrison, and from her earliest days, she brought joy and laughter to all who knew
8 her; and
9 WHEREAS, she was baptized at an early age and joined Hopewell African Methodist
10 Episcopal Church in Havana, Florida, where she developed a foundation of faith that
11 remained with her throughout her life; and
12 WHEREAS, she was united in holy matrimony to Robert Dickey, and together they were
13 blessed with one son, Robert Dickey Jr., who was the pride and joy of her life and whom she
14 loved with all her heart; and

15 WHEREAS, Betty Lee was known throughout her family as a devoted and loving mother
16 whose affection for her son was boundless, and she often spoke proudly of him and
17 celebrated every accomplishment and milestone in his life; and
18 WHEREAS, she also shared a special bond with her niece, Mary Ann Harrison, whom she
19 loved dearly and cared for as though she were her own child, offering guidance,
20 encouragement, and motherly affection that left a lasting impact; and
21 WHEREAS, understanding the value of hard work and perseverance, Mrs. Dickey labored
22 in the tobacco fields during her younger years and later worked as a domestic employee
23 before obtaining employment with the State of Florida, demonstrating determination and
24 dedication in every endeavor she pursued; and
25 WHEREAS, despite the challenges of balancing work and family responsibilities, she
26 remained committed to her education and proudly earned her diploma from Carter-Parramore
27 High School in Quincy, Florida, through the school's night program in 1968, serving as an
28 example of persistence and lifelong learning; and
29 WHEREAS, Betty Lee possessed a vibrant spirit and infectious personality, and she was
30 known as a fun-loving individual who enjoyed life, treasured family gatherings, and brought
31 warmth, laughter, and happiness to those fortunate enough to be in her presence; and
32 WHEREAS, although illness came upon her at a relatively young age and required her to
33 spend several years in nursing care facilities in Wakulla County and later Tallahassee,
34 Florida, she faced her circumstances with courage, dignity, and grace; and

35 WHEREAS, on October 26, 1976, Mrs. Betty Lee Harrison Dickey departed this earthly life
36 and entered into eternal rest, leaving behind treasured memories and a legacy of love that
37 continues to live on in the hearts of her family and friends; and
38 WHEREAS, her life serves as a testament to the enduring values of faith, perseverance,
39 devotion to family, and finding joy even in the face of adversity; and
40 WHEREAS, it is abundantly fitting and proper that the memory of this beloved daughter,
41 wife, mother, aunt, church member, and friend be appropriately recognized and honored.
42 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
43 the members of this body honor the life and memory of Betty Lee Harrison Dickey and
44 express their deepest respect and appreciation for a life that touched countless hearts through
45 kindness, faith, resilience, and love.
46 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
47 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
48 family of Betty Lee Harrison Dickey.
