26 LC 112 4324
House Resolution 37EX
of the 74th, Carter of the 93rd, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring the life and memory of Bishop Reginald Thomas Jackson; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, Bishop Reginald Thomas Jackson, a native of Dover, Delaware, was called to
3 ministry at the age of 18, pursued his theological education at Turner Theological Seminary
4 at the Interdenominational Theological Center in Atlanta, Georgia, and was ordained an
5 Itinerant Elder in 1977, beginning a ministry that would span more than five decades and
6 leave an indelible mark on the State of Georgia; and
7 WHEREAS, Bishop Jackson was elected and consecrated the 132nd Bishop of the African
8 Methodist Episcopal Church at the 49th Quadrennial Session of the General Conference held
9 in Nashville, Tennessee, in July 2012, ascending to one of the highest offices in America's
10 oldest Black denomination, which was founded by the Reverend Richard Allen in
11 Philadelphia in 1787; and
12 WHEREAS, from 2016 to 2024, Bishop Jackson served as the Presiding Prelate of the Sixth
13 Episcopal District of the African Methodist Episcopal Church, providing spiritual leadership,
14 moral vision, and prophetic witness to a community of more than 500 churches across the
15 State of Georgia; and
H. R. 37EX
- 1 -
26 LC 112 4324
16 WHEREAS, Bishop Jackson served as Chairman of the Board of Morris Brown College and
17 Turner Theological Seminary in Atlanta, and under his steadfast leadership, the Sixth
18 Episcopal District retired all of its institutional debts and Morris Brown College regained full
19 accreditation in 2022, preserving an irreplaceable pillar of Historically Black College and
20 University education for the citizens of Georgia; and
21 WHEREAS, Bishop Jackson was a relentless and courageous champion of hate crimes
22 legislation in Georgia, renewing his public call for its passage in the wake of the murder of
23 Ahmaud Arbery and countless others, until Georgia's Hate Crimes Legislation was signed
24 into law in 2020, providing historic protections for the people of this state; and
25 WHEREAS, recognizing that access to the ballot box is the cornerstone of American
26 democracy, Bishop Jackson launched Operation Voter Turnout, a comprehensive statewide
27 mobilization initiative requiring every African Methodist Episcopal congregation in Georgia
28 to establish voter registration committees ensuring that every eligible voter in the pews was
29 registered, educated on the issues, and ready to exercise their constitutional right to vote; and
30 WHEREAS, Bishop Jackson was among the most prominent and courageous opponents of
31 Senate Bill 202, the Georgia Election Integrity Act of 2021, calling it an attempt to "turn
32 back time to Jim Crow," leading the Sixth Episcopal District of the African Methodist
33 Episcopal Church to join as a plaintiff in federal litigation challenging provisions of the law,
34 and working alongside the NAACP Legal Defense Fund to protect the voting rights of Black
35 Georgians; and
36 WHEREAS, in 2022, Bishop Jackson founded Faith Works, a historic multidenominational
37 coalition uniting African American faith leaders from across Georgia representing more than
H. R. 37EX
- 2 -
26 LC 112 4324
38 1,000 churches and hundreds of thousands of parishioners in a unified effort to protect voting
39 access and counter voter suppression, declaring "Faith without works is dead"; and
40 WHEREAS, Bishop Jackson invoked his moral authority to call on major Georgia-based
41 corporations, including Coca-Cola, Delta Air Lines, and Home Depot, to take unambiguous
42 public positions against voter suppression legislation, demonstrating that prophetic leadership
43 demands accountability from all institutions with power to shape the common good; and
44 WHEREAS, beyond his voting rights leadership, Bishop Jackson was an outspoken advocate
45 for quality healthcare access for all Georgians, a living wage, education equity, and criminal
46 justice reform, declaring that "school ought not to be the pipeline to prison" and embodying
47 the fullness of a social gospel that served body, mind, and spirit; and
48 WHEREAS, Bishop Reginald Thomas Jackson entered eternal rest on November 25, 2025,
49 at the age of 71, in Washington, D.C., and upon his passing, the NAACP Legal Defense Fund
50 declared that "his leadership during the fight against voter suppression helped protect the
51 fundamental right to vote for countless Black Georgians" and the African Methodist
52 Episcopal Church honored his "extraordinary legacy of preaching, teaching, advocacy, and
53 servant leadership"; and
54 WHEREAS, Bishop Reginald Thomas Jackson is survived by his beloved children, Regina
55 Victoria Jackson and Seth Joshua Jackson, and by the thousands of Georgians whose lives,
56 communities, and democratic participation are forever enriched by his witness and sacrifice.
57 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
58 the members of this body join in honoring the extraordinary life, service, and legacy of
59 Bishop Reginald Thomas Jackson, 132nd elected and consecrated Bishop of the African

61 the sacred right to vote, and a towering figure in the civic and spiritual history of the State
62 of Georgia.
63 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
64 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
65 family of Bishop Reginald Thomas Jackson.
