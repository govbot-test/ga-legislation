26 LC 112 4278
House Resolution 18EX
By: Representatives Beckles of the 96th, Clark of the 108th, McClain of the 109th, Park of the
107th, Mitchell of the 88th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Commending the City of Duluth upon the occasion of its 150th Anniversary; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, the City of Duluth was founded in 1876 and has proudly served as a cornerstone
4 of Gwinnett County for 150 years, growing from a small railroad community into one of
5 Georgia's most vibrant and thriving municipalities; and
6 WHEREAS, throughout its rich history, Duluth has demonstrated a steadfast commitment
7 to progress, innovation, and community engagement while preserving the values and
8 traditions that have shaped its unique identity; and
9 WHEREAS, under the leadership of Mayor Greg Whitlock and the members of the Duluth
10 City Council—Marline Thomas, Lamar Doss, Manfred Graeder, Shenee Johnson-Cummings,
11 and Sarah Park—the City of Duluth has continued to advance a vision of responsible growth,
12 responsive government, and strong community engagement while fostering opportunities for
13 residents, businesses, and visitors alike; and
14 WHEREAS, the City of Duluth has emerged as a major economic engine within Gwinnett
15 County and the State of Georgia, attracting businesses, supporting entrepreneurship,

16 promoting tourism and entertainment, and creating economic opportunities that contribute
17 significantly to the prosperity of the region; and
18 WHEREAS, Duluth's rich multicultural heritage and diverse population reflect the strength
19 of Georgia's communities, bringing together people of different backgrounds, cultures, and
20 experiences to build a vibrant, inclusive, and welcoming city that enriches the social and
21 economic fabric of Gwinnett County; and
22 WHEREAS, generations of residents, business owners, civic leaders, educators, public
23 servants, and volunteers have contributed to the growth and success of the City of Duluth,
24 creating a community known for its diversity, resilience, and welcoming spirit; and
25 WHEREAS, Duluth has become a destination for families, entrepreneurs, artists, and visitors
26 alike through its vibrant downtown district, thriving business community, outstanding parks
27 and recreational facilities, cultural events, and commitment to quality of life; and
28 WHEREAS, the city continues to play a significant role in the economic, cultural, and civic
29 vitality of Gwinnett County and the State of Georgia; and
30 WHEREAS, on September 12, 2026, residents and visitors will gather to celebrate "The Big
31 150: Off the Rails," commemorating a century and a half of achievement, growth, and
32 community pride while honoring the city's past and embracing its future; and
33 WHEREAS, it is abundantly fitting and proper that the outstanding accomplishments and
34 enduring contributions of the City of Duluth be appropriately honored.
